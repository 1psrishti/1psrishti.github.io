Self Discovery

"The journey to self love begins with self discovery."


Personal development has been growing in popularity within the last couple of years. Whether it’s books, podcasts, or inspirational documentaries, people are seeking a better understanding of who they are and how they can improve their life. A huge part of this personal growth movement is self-discovery.


Traditionally, self-discovery is simply known as discovering something about yourself, which is easy enough to understand. Most people associate self-discovery with taking a personality test or doing some soul searching to figure out your dream career.


However, self-discovery goes way beyond just finding out your personality type or your perfect job. In fact, self-discovery is almost every aspect of personal development.


Some of the aspects of self-discovery:

  1. Discovering your passions
  2. Figuring out your purpose
  3. Finding out what motivates you

Check out these 9 tips to start your self-discovery journey.

Attitude

The definition of an attitude is a way of feeling or acting toward a person, thing or situation. Passion for a sport, dislike for a certain actor and negativity toward life in general are each an example of an attitude.


Evaluate your current attitude. What attitudes make you feel most negative about yourself? What actions create conflict between you and others? What thoughts cloud or control your mind?


If you were trying to motivate other people, you’d pump them up, wouldn’t you? You’d offer words of support, encouragement and inspiration.


Do you do the same for yourself?


I can’t. I doubt. I don’t think. I don’t have the time. I’m afraid. I don’t believe.


This self-doubt darkens our attitudes, limits our success and casts a shadow over our lives. The fix is easy: Change the language. I can. I expect the best. I know. I’ll make the time. I am confident. I believe.


Rewire your thought patterns. Our feelings come from our thoughts. We can change them by changing our thought patterns. It’s our thoughts, not our circumstances, that determine our happiness.

Some concepts to find yourself, and create a better version of yourself :

SWOT Analysis

A SWOT analysis is a compilation of your strengths, weaknesses, opportunities and threats. It provides insights based on your personality strengths and weaknesses, what challenges you see ahead of you, and what opportunities are present around you now and in the future. Strengths and opportunities are things you consider favorable and within your control, while weaknesses and threats are unfavorable and dictated by external forces. You can use this data to explore the correlation between them, how to leverage your strengths to make the most of your opportunities, and how to improve weaknesses to mitigate threats.

Ikigai Test

Ikigai is a Japanese word that translates as a reason for being, a reason to get up in the morning. It’s the combination of IKI, meaning life, and KAI, meaning the realisation of hope and expectations. Ikigai combines the joy from doing something and a sense of purpose, meaning and well-being. It’s feeling that your life is valuable, that you have an impact. It’s not linked to your status in society, or the money you earn. Everyone’s Ikigai is unique: it’s based on your personal history, your values, your beliefs and your personality. It’s a personal journey that may take time.

Personal Branding

Personal branding is the conscious and intentional effort to create and influence public perception of an individual by differentiating themselves from the competition, to ultimately advance their career, increase their circle of influence, and have a larger impact. The process of personal branding involves finding your uniqueness, building a reputation on the things you want to be known for, and then allowing yourself to be known for them. Ultimately, the goal is to create something that conveys a message and that can be monetized.

Johari Window

Johari Window is a technique for improving self-awareness within an individual. It helps in understanding your relationship with yourself and others. Johari Window model can be a useful tool if you want to improve your communication skills. Generally, it is believed that we have in a group based on our perception of others. And that is why to improve communication in a group it is important to develop the perception of an individual in the group. The model is based upon two things – to acquire the trust of others by revealing your information to them and by learning about yourself through feedback by others.

💕 Here are some activities related to self-discovery and attitude :

Ask why three times.


Whenever you feel down, play this game. Ask yourself a 'why' question, answer it, and then ask another connected 'why' question to yourself.


Why do I hate my job? Because I feel tired all day.
Why do I feel tired at the office all day? Because the air is really bad.
Why is the air really bad? Because we never open a window.



Turn your opinions and thoughts into questions.


This activity can help reduce negativity from your life. Whenever you make negative opinion about someone or something, simply turn that opinion into a question by adding a question-mark at the end of it.


So instead of thinking "I'm bad at maths." think "I'm bad at maths?"
Instead of thinking "Brandon is an idiot." think "Brandon is an idiot?"
